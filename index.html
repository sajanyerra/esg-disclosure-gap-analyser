Project Overview

What This Project Does

ESG risk analysis is a core function at firms like MSCI, Sustainalytics and Bloomberg ESG. Analysts assess not just what companies claim about their sustainability practices — but what the data actually shows about their unmanaged risk exposure. This project builds that process as a complete data pipeline focused on the US Energy sector.

🐍

Data Pipeline

Python and Pandas used to ingest, clean, and analyse S&P 500 ESG Risk Ratings data across 20 energy companies.

🐘

PostgreSQL Database

Three relational tables storing company data, ESG scores, and industry-level summaries — queried with SQL.

📊

Tableau Dashboard

Interactive dashboard with risk leaderboard, score breakdown, industry comparison and environment driver analysis.

🔍

ESG Risk Scoring

Sustainalytics methodology — measuring unmanaged ESG risk across Environmental, Social and Governance dimensions.

Analysis Results

Key Findings

The analysis reveals significant variation in ESG risk across Oil & Gas sub-industries, with environment consistently dominating as the primary risk driver.

41.7

Highest ESG Risk — Occidental Petroleum

Rated Severe. Occidental's direct extraction operations across the full E&P value chain drive the highest environmental risk score in the dataset at 25.0.

19.0

Lowest ESG Risk — Kinder Morgan

Rated Low. The only company in the dataset to achieve a Low classification. Midstream pipeline operations carry significantly lower direct environmental exposure.

85%

Environment Dominates Risk

17 of 20 companies have Environment as their dominant risk driver — reflecting the sector's inherent carbon transition and pollution liability exposure.

39.1

Integrated Oil Carries Most Risk

Oil & Gas Integrated companies average 39.1 — the highest of any sub-industry. Operating across the full value chain accumulates ESG risk at every stage.

24.8

Midstream Carries Least Risk

Oil & Gas Midstream averages just 24.8. Pipeline and infrastructure operators have lower direct emissions exposure than extraction companies.

75%

Majority Rated High or Severe

15 of 20 companies carry High or Severe ESG risk. Only one company — Kinder Morgan — achieves a Low risk classification.

Methodology

How ESG Risk is Measured

This project uses the Sustainalytics ESG Risk Rating methodology — the same framework used by institutional investors managing trillions in assets. The core concept is unmanaged risk, not performance rating.

E

Environment Risk Score

Measures exposure to carbon transition risk, pollution liability, resource depletion and climate physical risk — minus what the company is actively managing.

env_risk = exposure − managed_risk
S

Social Risk Score

Measures labour practice risk, supply chain exposure, community relations and human capital management — particularly relevant for Midstream operators.

soc_risk = exposure − managed_risk
G

Governance Risk Score

Measures board independence, executive compensation structures, shareholder rights and transparency of corporate reporting practices.

gov_risk = exposure − managed_risk

Negligible

Minimal unmanaged risk

Score 0 — 10

Low

Well managed risk

Score 10 — 20

Medium

Moderate risk exposure

Score 20 — 30

High

Significant unmanaged risk

Score 30 — 40

Limitations of This Analysis

Acknowledging limitations is a sign of analytical maturity. Every ESG dataset and methodology has constraints.

1

Single Point in Time

This dataset represents one snapshot. Multi-year trend analysis would reveal whether companies are improving or worsening their ESG risk management over time.

2

One Rating Agency View

Scores reflect Sustainalytics methodology only. MSCI, Bloomberg ESG and CDP may rate the same companies differently — ESG rating divergence is a known issue.

3

US Listed Companies Only

Shell, BP and TotalEnergies are absent as they are not S&P 500 constituents. A global dataset would provide a more complete picture of the energy sector.

4

Two Companies Excluded

Diamondback Energy and Baker Hughes were removed due to missing ESG scores in the source dataset. This slightly reduces the sample size.

5

Self-Reported Inputs

Some data feeding into Sustainalytics scores originates from company sustainability disclosures which may themselves have limitations in accuracy or completeness.
